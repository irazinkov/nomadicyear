Everyone starts with the dual battery, so why be original?

Read everything I could find about this on the internet. Bought everything I thought I would need, rolled up my sleeves and went to work.

If you want to work on your truck this is easy to do. All you need is a drill, screwdrivers and patience.

Now people also integrate a solar panel and a charge controller. I want to do this so BAD, will see if the time and money allow this before we leave.

&nbsp;

Skip ahead to the photos because some boring tech info is coming.

Here is what you need for this:
<ol>
	<li>Figure out the battery that you will use - DieHard Marine Deep Cycle (x2)</li>
	<li>Where are you going to put them - on a 2000 Land Cruiser both fit under the hood</li>
	<li>Get a battery tray</li>
	<li>Voltage sensitive relay - it disconnects the batteries during discharge to allow only one to drain ( Blue Sea MC-ACR)</li>
	<li>Circuit breaker to disconnect the power cable coming out of the second battery (Blue Sea 187-series 200A circuit breaker)</li>
	<li>Welding cable - it is flexible enough even at larger gauges</li>
	<li>Lugs, heatshrink, wire loom...</li>
	<li>Power bus (Positive + Ground) - Blue Sea 2722</li>
	<li>I put the power bus in the rear right of the truck and ran the cable through the firewall and under the carpet.</li>
</ol>
